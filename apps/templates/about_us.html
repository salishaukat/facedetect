FindMeBahamas, a digital search database for missing persons developed out of concern for the scale of missing persons after Hurricane Dorian; a Category 5 hurricane that ripped through two (2) major islands in The Bahamas, Abaco & Grand Bahama.

FindMeBahamas leverages facial recognition and blockchain to match missing persons based on photos uploaded by loved ones or, shelter officials. FindMeBahamas also generates for each user a 6 digit ID number, providing a digital ID for governments and shelters to cross check identities of persons.

CEO of Bluestone Technologies, John Bridgewater, rallied up his team to develop FindMeBahamas as a result of the terrifying experience of his missing grandmother, step-grandfather and brother after the storm.

A native of Grand Bahama Island, John felt a need to do something for when this occurs again. The Bahamas is geographically situated in a hurricane belt and is prone to yearly hurricanes. John sought a need to be ready for when it does occur again in The Bahamas and is currently building for when other neighboring countries experience the same.

These networks confirm the prowess of Bahamian technical expertise to contribute to social good.
